Recovering After Joint Replacement: Your Rehab Roadmap

Getting a joint replacement is a major step toward a pain-free life, but the surgery is only half the battle. The real work begins with rehabilitation. At Mehar Physiotherapy Clinic, I specialize in helping patients navigate this recovery journey.

Why Post-Op Physiotherapy Matters

Without proper exercise, the tissues around your new joint can become stiff, leading to a limited range of motion. Physiotherapy ensures:

  • Early Mobilization: Safely getting you on your feet as soon as possible.
  • Swelling Management: Using specific movements and techniques to reduce inflammation.
  • Muscle Strengthening: Building the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes to support your new joint.

To understand the surgical side of joint recovery, visit the AAOS OrthoInfo guide.

What to Expect in Your Sessions

Initially, we focus on gentle movements to prevent blood clots and stiffness. As you progress, we move to functional training—like climbing stairs and walking without aids—to ensure you can return to your life with confidence.
