Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about services, sessions, home visits and bookings.

Treatment is provided for back and neck pain, joint pain, arthritis, sports injuries, post-surgical rehabilitation, neurological conditions, pediatric cases, and age-related mobility issues.

No referral is required. Appointments can be booked directly.

A typical session lasts 30 to 45 minutes, depending on the treatment plan and patient condition.

The number of sessions depends on the severity of the condition, treatment goals, and progress. A personalized treatment plan is prepared after the first assessment.

Physiotherapy is designed to reduce pain and improve mobility. Some exercises may cause mild discomfort initially, but treatments remain safe and comfortable.

Bring any medical reports, X-rays, MRI/CT scans, prescriptions, or doctor’s notes, along with comfortable clothing for movement and exercise.

Home physiotherapy sessions can be arranged depending on the condition and location. Please contact for details.

Strict hygiene, sanitization, and sterilization protocols are maintained to provide a safe environment for all patients.

Physiotherapy helps seniors with balance, flexibility, arthritis management, fall prevention, and post-surgery recovery, allowing them to stay independent and active.

Appointments can be booked online through the website or by calling directly.

Each treatment plan is tailored to the patient’s condition, goals, and lifestyle to ensure effective recovery and long-term results.

Sports injury management includes sprains, strains, ligament injuries, and rehabilitation to help patients return to activity safely.

Physiotherapy supports recovery after surgeries such as joint replacement, fracture repair, or spinal procedures, improving mobility and reducing pain.

Follow-up sessions and home exercises are often recommended to maintain improvements and prevent recurrence of pain or injury.

Treatment may include manual therapy, mobilization techniques, PNF (Proprioceptive Neuromuscular Facilitation), gait analysis, and exercise programs designed for specific conditions.

Patients may be advised to perform specific home exercises, avoid certain movements temporarily, and follow posture or activity guidelines to support recovery and prevent injury.
